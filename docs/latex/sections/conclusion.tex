\section{Conclusion}\label{sec:conclusion}
This work establishes a full-ionic ePC-SAFT parameterization for modeling loaded aqueous \MEA with explicit true-species chemistry.  Literature VLE and NMR speciation data were assembled on a common loading basis, transformed into a charge-balanced liquid representation, and evaluated through ePC-SAFT calculations that include activity-coupled reaction equilibria, electrolyte fugacity calculations, concentration-dependent permittivity, and SSM+DS Born solvation options.

The validation calculations show that the historical six-species pressure baseline is reproducible, that neutral ePC-SAFT reproduces the corresponding neutral PC-SAFT pressure path, and that the promoted full ionic parameter set can evaluate both pressure and speciation data sets without state failures.  The ionic evaluation solved all 161 pressure records and all 74 speciation records.  The \COtwo pressure comparison has a median absolute logarithmic residual of 0.331, while the fitted amine-family ions show substantially smaller liquid-composition deviations: the median absolute logarithmic residuals are 0.087 for \MEAH and 0.041 for \MEACOO.

The strongest parameter claim is therefore specific rather than universal.  \MEAH and \MEACOO have been fitted against direct MEA-system speciation data and are no longer seed-only values.  The carbonate-family and water autoionization ions are included in the full species basis, with literature-backed or diagnostic parameter status, but they are not independently SSM+DS-fitted in this work.  The regularized trace-carbonate fit retains $d^{\mathrm{Born}}=3.0$ for both \HCOthree and \COthree, while an unanchored multistart diagnostic identifies a lower-residual bicarbonate-sensitive alternative that has not been promoted because the current package-native coupled candidate returned \texttt{package\_fit\_not\_completed}.  This distinction is important because trace \HthreeO and \Hydroxide mole fractions are controlled primarily by reaction equilibria and numerical floors, not by directly measured ion-composition targets.

Further pressure-weighted regression of the most important binary interactions may reduce the pressure residuals, provided that the fitted speciation quality is preserved.  Such a regression should report active bounds, parameter uncertainty or local sensitivity, objective-function improvement, pressure residual distributions, and species-resolved speciation residuals.  The present study provides the data basis, parameter evidence matrix, SSM+DS model configuration, and validation figures and tables needed for a final predictive ePC-SAFT parameter study for carbon-capture process simulation.
