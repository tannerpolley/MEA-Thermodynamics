\section{Results and Discussion}\label{sec:mea-system-modeling-results}
This section reports the parameterization and validation results for the \MEA--\COtwo--\HtwoO system.  The presentation follows the structure used in ePC-SAFT amine-solubility studies: data basis, baseline checks, parameter tables, pressure validation, speciation validation, and regression interpretation.

\subsection{Experimental Data Basis}
The VLE data set contains 161 \COtwo partial-pressure records from Aronu et al., Hilliard, Jou et al., Mamun et al., and Xu et al.\cite{Aronu2011,Hilliard2008,Jou1995,Mamun2005,Xu2011}.  Each record contains amine mass fraction, temperature, \COtwo loading, and measured \COtwo pressure.  The speciation data set contains 74 NMR-derived liquid-composition records from Boettinger et al., Jakobsen et al., and Matin et al.\cite{Bottinger2008,Jakobsen2005,Matin2012}.  The data are expressed on a common loading basis and reconciled to satisfy carbon, amine, water, and charge balances before ePC-SAFT evaluation.

\subsection{Baseline and Neutral ePC-SAFT Checks}
Two baseline checks were used before interpreting the full ionic model.  First, the legacy six-species chemistry path was regenerated for 30 wt\% \MEA.  It includes \COtwo, \MEA, \HtwoO, \MEAH, \MEACOO, \HCOthree, and the aggregate $\MEA+\MEAH$.  The pressure comparison against the Jou 30 wt\% data gives median absolute logarithmic residuals of approximately 0.39, 0.31, 0.15, 0.06, and 0.08 at 40, 60, 80, 100, and \SI{120}{\degreeCelsius}, respectively.  This reproduces the expected pressure trend and provides continuity with the older PC-SAFT path.

Second, the neutral ePC-SAFT pressure calculation was compared directly with the legacy PC-SAFT pressure output.  The neutral parity check demonstrates that the ePC-SAFT package can reproduce the neutral pressure path when the electrolyte terms are not the controlling difference.  This isolates the later ionic comparisons from simple package-interface or unit-conversion errors.

\subsection{Full-Ionic ePC-SAFT Parameter Set}
The full ionic liquid basis contains nine species: \COtwo, \MEA, \HtwoO, \MEAH, \MEACOO, \HCOthree, \COthree, \HthreeO, and \Hydroxide.  The promoted parameter set is shown in \cref{tab:full-ionic-ssm-ds-parameters}.  It combines inherited neutral parameters, SSM+DS ion conventions from the modified-Born ePC-SAFT literature, and direct MEA-system regression for the two amine-family ions that are best supported by the NMR/speciation data.  The parameter-evidence matrix in \cref{tab:parameter-evidence-matrix} is included to prevent over-interpreting weakly identifiable trace ions as independently regressed species.

\input{tables/full_ionic_ssm_ds_parameters}

The binary-interaction matrix retains $k_{\MEA,\HtwoO}=-0.052$ from the inherited molecular path and promotes the real-data fitted value $k_{\MEAH,\MEACOO}=-0.002018$.  Literature water--ion interactions are also retained for the proton/carbonate family: $k_{\HtwoO,\HthreeO}=0.25$, $k_{\HtwoO,\Hydroxide}=-0.25$, $k_{\HtwoO,\HCOthree}=0$, and $k_{\HtwoO,\COthree}=-0.25$.  The remaining MEA-specific ion--molecule and ion--ion terms are held at zero for this validation set.  This sparse interaction structure tests the predictive value of the full ionic activity model and the SSM+DS Born treatment before introducing a heavily tuned binary-interaction matrix.

\subsection{Full-Ionic Pressure and Speciation Evaluation}
The full ionic ePC-SAFT evaluation solves all 161 assembled VLE records and all 74 speciation records without recorded state failures.  The pressure residuals have a median absolute value of 0.332 on the $\log_{10}(P_{\mathrm{model}}/P_{\mathrm{data}})$ scale, with a maximum absolute value of 0.977.  These values show that the promoted parameter set has the correct order of magnitude across the literature data.  They also show that the parameterization should be interpreted as a full-ionic validation set rather than as a final pressure-optimized model.

The speciation comparison is shown in \cref{fig:ionic-speciation}.  Major amine-family species are reproduced more closely than trace molecular \COtwo, hydronium, and hydroxide.  The median absolute logarithmic model/data deviations are approximately 0.171 for \MEA, 0.0088 for \HtwoO, 0.0947 for \MEAH, 0.0594 for \MEACOO, 0.479 for \HCOthree, and 0.860 for \COthree.  The much larger trace-species deviations for molecular \COtwo, \HthreeO, and \Hydroxide reflect the difficulty of comparing species that are either indirectly inferred or present near the numerical floor.  They are therefore diagnostic residuals rather than the primary regression-quality claim.

\subsection{Ion-Parameter Regression Status and Interpretation}
The promoted ion-parameter regression uses the direct Tier A speciation subset from Matin et al., Jakobsen et al., and Boettinger et al.\cite{Matin2012,Jakobsen2005,Bottinger2008}.  The active fitted variables are $\sigma$, $\epsilon/k$, and $d^{\mathrm{Born}}$ for \MEAH and \MEACOO, plus $k_{\MEAH,\MEACOO}$.  The optimizer terminated successfully on the eight-row promoted subset, decreasing the residual norm from 0.2714 to 0.2677 and improving the median absolute $\log_{10}(\mathrm{model}/\mathrm{data})$ error from 0.1010 to 0.0988.  The fitted \MEAH $R^2$ improved from 0.655 to 0.665, and the fitted \MEACOO $R^2$ improved from 0.888 to 0.905.  No promoted fit parameter is at a bound.

The carbonate-family and trace-water ions have a different evidence status.  \HCOthree and \COthree are observed in the speciation data, so their SSM+DS Born radii were tested against all Tier A rows that report either species.  A direct full-data scan retained $d_{\HCOthree}^{\mathrm{Born}}=3.0$ and $d_{\COthree}^{\mathrm{Born}}=3.0$ as the lowest-residual pair among the tested candidates; the residual norm increased from 0.702 at 3.0/3.0 to 0.838 at 4.5/4.5, 1.061 at 6.5/7.5, and 1.913 at 1.05/1.05.  The available bicarbonate/carbonate targets therefore do not support a substantially different Born diameter in this parameter set.  \HthreeO and \Hydroxide are present mainly through charge balance and water autoionization and are not independently identifiable from the available NMR targets.  They are therefore retained as literature-backed SSM+DS/proton-activity values rather than reported as independently regressed species.
