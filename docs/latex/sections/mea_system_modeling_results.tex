\section{Results and Discussion}\label{sec:mea-system-modeling-results}
The main result is a fixed-parameter SSM+DS modified-Born ePC-SAFT evaluation of reactive \MEA \COtwo solubility against both pressure and speciation data.  The ideal baseline verifies the reaction and balance conventions, and the activity-coupled ePC-SAFT calculation converges on the assembled \COtwo-solubility pressure and speciation records with explicit residuals.  \MEA, \MEAH, and \MEACOO are the strongest supported liquid observables.  Bicarbonate, carbonate, and trace water ions remain limiting observables, and the pressure residuals are reported as a fixed activity-based \COtwo-solubility evaluation rather than a pressure-optimized model.

\subsection{Ideal CO2 Solubility Baseline}
The ideal Smith--Missen-style baseline uses the same five-reaction, nine-species liquid equilibrium problem with activities set equal to mole fractions.  It provides a convention check for the reaction set before ePC-SAFT activity coefficients are introduced.  For major observed species, the overall median absolute $\log_{10}(\mathrm{model}/\mathrm{data})$ residuals are 0.055 for \MEA, 0.044 for \MEAH, 0.029 for \MEACOO, 0.227 for \HCOthree, and 0.0087 for the aggregate \ce{MEA + MEAH+}.  Molecular \COtwo and \COthree are solved and plotted, but their residual rows are not used as major-species evidence.

The corresponding solubility-pressure baseline gives an overall median absolute $\log_{10}$ pressure residual of 0.160.  The temperature-resolved pressure comparison is uneven: the 40 and \SI{60}{\degreeCelsius} rows have larger residuals, whereas the 80, 100, and \SI{120}{\degreeCelsius} rows have smaller residuals.  The baseline is therefore used as a reproducibility and convention check, not as the final activity-based solubility result.

\subsection{Activity-Based CO2 Solubility Model}
The fixed activity-based evaluation uses the nine-species liquid basis and parameter hierarchy reported in \cref{sec:data-methods}.  The parameter set combines inherited neutral molecular values, SSM+DS conventions for ions, provisional fixed inputs for \MEAH and \MEACOO, and trace-ion quantities retained as literature-backed or sensitivity-limited inputs.  This places the modified-Born transfer at the center of the \COtwo-solubility calculation: as loading changes, the model updates the ionic composition, the dielectric environment, and the solvation contribution used in the activity coefficients.  The thermodynamic implication is that \COtwo solubility pressure and speciation are evaluated together for a single fixed activity model, without treating the parameter set as a pressure-only VLE correlation or as a newly regressed parameterization.

\subsection{Chemical speciation}
The nonzero measured-target speciation metrics are 0.074 for \MEA, 0.033 for \MEAH, 0.040 for \MEACOO, 0.461 for \HCOthree, and 0.066 for the aggregate \ce{MEA + MEAH+}, all on the median absolute $\log_{10}(\mathrm{model}/\mathrm{data})$ scale.  Measurements reported as zero and treated as upper-bound targets remain below a maximum modeled \HCOthree mole fraction of 0.00128.  Carbonate nonzero measured targets have a larger median residual of 0.599 and are retained as a limiting species family.

\subsection{CO2 Solubility Pressure Response}
The true-species ePC-SAFT activity calculation converged for all 161 assembled \COtwo-solubility VLE records and all 74 speciation records.  The pressure residual is a median absolute $\log_{10}(P_{\mathrm{model}}/P_{\mathrm{data}})$ value of 0.493 across the solubility data.  Because \(10^{0.493}\approx 3.1\), the median pressure error corresponds to about a factor of 3.1.  This supports fixed-parameter \COtwo-solubility modeling with quantified residuals, not a pressure-optimized VLE correlation.

Temperature-resolved pressure errors remain largest between 40 and \SI{80}{\degreeCelsius} and decrease at \SI{120}{\degreeCelsius}.  The paired pressure and speciation accounting is central to the result: the pressure comparison tests gas--liquid equilibrium, while the NMR residuals test whether the liquid chemistry supporting that pressure remains plausible.  The pressure comparison therefore quantifies the fixed activity-based solubility calculation without treating it as a completed pressure/speciation regression.

\subsection{Sensitivity and identifiability}
\HCOthree and \COthree are observed in the speciation data, but their parameter sensitivity is less stable than that of the amine-family observables.  This is a useful identifiability result for SSM+DS transfer: the NMR data test the amine-family states directly, whereas carbonate-family Born diameters remain less constrained by the available measurements.  The retained calculation uses \(d_{\HCOthree}^{\mathrm{Born}}=3.0\) and \(d_{\COthree}^{\mathrm{Born}}=3.0\).  A separate unanchored sensitivity check finds a lower residual near \(d_{\HCOthree}^{\mathrm{Born}}=6.80\) and \(d_{\COthree}^{\mathrm{Born}}=3.00\).  That alternative is not used in the reported activity-based evaluation because it is conditioned only on carbonate-family residuals and is not part of a completed pressure/speciation regression.  \HthreeO and \Hydroxide are controlled mainly by charge balance and water autoionization and are not independently identifiable from the available NMR targets.

\subsection{Comparison with literature models}
The literature comparison in \cref{tab:literature-model-comparison} separates apparent-chemistry PC-SAFT treatments, explicit-ion ePC-SAFT amine models, and advanced Born electrolyte models for gas solubility.  The present calculation follows the explicit-ion activity-coupled route while adding the SSM+DS modified-Born treatment to a carbamate-forming \MEA system.  The validation structure follows prior explicit-ion ePC-SAFT amine-solubility studies that separate parameter provenance from final VLE checks, but it extends the comparison by placing measured \MEA speciation residuals beside the solubility-pressure residuals~\cite{Uyan2015,Wangler2018}.

\subsection{Model limitations and transferability}
Because the parameter values were selected before the present fixed-parameter evaluation but not by a reproducible current regression, source-stratified residuals are descriptive diagnostics rather than held-out validation.  A reduced finite-difference sensitivity screen identifies \(k_{\MEA,\HtwoO}\), \(k_{\COtwo,\MEA}\), and \(d_{\COthree}^{\mathrm{Born}}\) as the strongest tracked directions in the reported objective neighborhood, while \(d_{\HthreeO}^{\mathrm{Born}}\) remains near-zero on the reported metrics.  Future fitting should move binary interactions and carbonate Born terms only within a preregistered multi-observable design that preserves amine-speciation behavior and reserves independent data for validation.
