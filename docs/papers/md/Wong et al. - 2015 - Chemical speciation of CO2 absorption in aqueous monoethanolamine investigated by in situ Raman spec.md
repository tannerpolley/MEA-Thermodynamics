\begin{abstract}
A quantitative analysis method of chemical species in liquid phase during $\mathrm{CO}_{2}$ absorption into monoethanolamine was developed for Raman spectroscopy. Band envelopes in the spectral region between 300 and $3100 \mathrm{~cm}^{-1}$ were resolved with Gaussian Lorentzian function. Spectroscopic characterization of MEA, protonated MEA, carbamate, bicarbonate, carbonate and molecular $\mathrm{CO}_{2}$ in aqueous was performed. Calibration curves were constructed with area ratio plotted against species concentration. Concentration of individual chemical species in liquid phase can be systematically determined using the correlations introduced. Experiments of $\mathrm{CO}_{2}$ solubility in aqueous MEA were carried out for pressure ranges from 1 to 60 bar and three temperatures ( $303.15,313.15$ and 323.15 ) K with in situ Raman measurement. Absorption capacity was determined with conventional gas pressure drop method and newly developed Raman spectroscopic analysis method based on total carbon containing species in liquid phase. Results depict good accuracy and reproducibility of Raman measurement method. Chemical speciation during $\mathrm{CO}_{2}$ absorption into aqueous solution of $30 \%$ MEA at pressure up to 60 bar was evaluated. The findings from present work will improve the understanding of phase equilibrium behavior associated with absorption reactions and can be employed for monitoring, analysis and optimization of $\mathrm{CO}_{2}$ capture process.
\end{abstract}

\section*{1. Introduction}

In recent years, it has been shown that carbon dioxide ( $\mathrm{CO}_{2}$ ) is one of the major factors accelerating global warming. It is thus crucial to develop and employ efficient methods for acid gas treating. Apart from the recognition of greenhouse gas, $\mathrm{CO}_{2}$ is also known as acid gas, which is one of the impurities in natural gas. $\mathrm{CO}_{2}$ reacts with water to form carbonic acid that is corrosive to pipeline and equipment. Besides, $\mathrm{CO}_{2}$ removal is important to increase heating value of natural gas and optimize pipeline capacity. Absorption is the most established technique to separate $\mathrm{CO}_{2}$ and amines have long been identified as commercial solvents for removal of carbon dioxide in natural gas industry. Aqueous MEA is a popular choice in amine scrubbing technology because of its strong base nature and fast reactivity with $\mathrm{CO}_{2}$ (Akanksha et al., 2007).

Thermodynamic and mass transfer models are important elements in designing and operating $\mathrm{CO}_{2}$ removal systems, thus accurate determination of the equilibrium of $\mathrm{CO}_{2}$ in aqueous amine is of major interest for both technical and economic consider-

Many studies were conducted on vapor-liquid equilibria in amine-water- $\mathrm{CO}_{2}$ systems based on pressure change in gas phase, but quantitative analysis on the liquid phase speciation is relatively scarce in literature (Singh et al., 2010; Xu et al., 1998). Besides, characterization of individual species in aqueous phase provides crucial input for simulation of process design and optimization. There are several methods to characterize liquid phase in aqueous alkonalamines. The earliest method used is titration, which is tedious and requires preparation of sample and analysis cannot be performed during the process of absorption. Sulaiman et al. developed a technique to determine concentration of all species at equilibrium based on neutralization of the carbonated amine solution with a strong base (Haji-Sulaiman et al., 1996).
${ }^{1} \mathrm{H}$ and ${ }^{13} \mathrm{C}$ NMR is a better analytical option compared to titration and optical techniques because no calibration is needed and structure of molecules obtained enables byproduct analysis. Plenty of studies on speciation study in liquid phase and determination of equilibrium constants using NMR spectroscopy were reported (Böttinger et al., 2008; Ciftja et al., 2013; Ermatchkov et al., 2003; Jakobsen et al., 2005). However long acquisition time for quantitative ${ }^{13} \mathrm{C}$ NMR experiments is not desirable for kinetic experiments, therefore some researchers opted for vibrational spectroscopy for liquid phase analysis. Among the early studies of FTIR spectroscopic
method to analyze the equilibrium concentrations of vapor and liquid phases in acid-gas-alkanolamine systems were conducted by Rogers (Rogers et al., 1998; Rogers et al., 1997). Researchers designed experimental apparatus specifically for online infrared spectroscopic investigations of the multicomponent species in acid gas reacted amine solutions (Archane et al., 2008; Diab et al., 2012).

Raman spectroscopy is a non-invasive, non-destructive and direct measurement method efficient for quantitative analysis for concentration as low as 0.1 up to $100 \%$. It is relatively fast, suitable for measurements in aqueous system because of the weak Raman scattering of water molecules and application of fiber optic probes allows easy handling for in situ reaction monitoring (Pelletier, 2003). Raman was used to characterize and measure species distribution in $\mathrm{CO}_{2}$ loaded ammonia solution (Kim et al., 2008; Wen and Brooker, 1995; Zhao et al., 2011). Several researchers published speciation data in $\mathrm{CO}_{2}$ loaded aqueous amine based solvent system. In situ Raman spectroscopy quantitative analysis was attained using multivariate regression algorithm (Souchon et al., 2011; Vogt et al., 2011). Accuracy of the method is questioned as absorption signal is affected by shot noise, instrument noise, and background light, which can come from sample, sample container and environment around the sample, hence it is susceptible to errors when a different Raman system or sample cell is used (Pelletier, 1999). Besides, it is very difficult to precisely control incident laser intensity of Raman spectroscopy resulting in variance of absorbance measured (Larkin, 2011). Species distribution of carbonated MEA, DEA and AMP solutions were analyzed recently using semi-quantitative speciation information from Raman spectra. However, characteristic band used to quantify MEA was not detected in bicarbonate loaded MEA solution and estimation of free amine concentration yielded error higher than $50 \%$ (Samarakoon et al., 2013).

Numerous ions are produced by chemical reactions of acid gas and aqueous alkanolamine solution. Equilibrium reactions that occur during $\mathrm{CO}_{2}$ absorption in aqueous solution of MEA are as represented in Eqs. (1)-(5).

Amine protonation:
MEA $+\mathrm{H}^{+} \leftrightarrow \mathrm{MEAH}^{+}$

Carbamate deformation:

$$
\begin{equation*}
\mathrm{MEACOO}^{-}+\mathrm{H}_{2} \mathrm{O} \leftrightarrow \mathrm{MEA}+\mathrm{HCO}_{3}{ }^{-} \tag{2}
\end{equation*}
$$


Carbon dioxide hydrolysis:

$$
\begin{equation*}
\mathrm{CO}_{2}+\mathrm{H}_{2} \mathrm{O} \leftrightarrow \mathrm{HCO}_{3}{ }^{-}+\mathrm{H}^{+} \tag{3}
\end{equation*}
$$


Water dissociation:

$$
\begin{equation*}
\mathrm{H}_{2} \mathrm{O} \leftrightarrow \mathrm{H}^{+}+\mathrm{OH}^{-} \tag{4}
\end{equation*}
$$


Bicarbonate ion dissociation:

$$
\begin{equation*}
\mathrm{HCO}_{3}{ }^{-} \leftrightarrow \mathrm{CO}_{3}{ }^{2-}+\mathrm{H}^{+} \tag{5}
\end{equation*}
$$


In this work, a Raman method was developed and used to quantify the equilibrium concentration of species present in $\mathrm{CO}_{2}$-MEA- $\mathrm{H}_{2} \mathrm{O}$ system. $\mathrm{CO}_{2}$ solubility in aqueous MEA was analyzed with conventional pressure drop method in vapor phase and Raman spectroscopic method based on total carbon containing species in liquid phase. The experimental matrix includes pressures in the range of 1-60 bar and temperatures from 303.15 K to 323.15 K at MEA concentration of 30 mass\%. Results from both methods were compared to evaluate accuracy and reproducibility of Raman measurement method. Chemical speciation during $\mathrm{CO}_{2}$ absorption into aqueous solution of MEA at elevated pressures was also studied.

\section*{2. Materials and methods}

\subsection*{2.1. Materials}
$\mathrm{CO}_{2}$ with purity of $99.8 \%$ was purchased from Air Product Malaysia Sdn. Bhd while MEA of reagent grade (purity $>99 \%$ ), sodium bicarbonate $\left(\mathrm{NaHCO}_{3}\right)$, sodium carbonate $\left(\mathrm{Na}_{2} \mathrm{CO}_{3}\right)$, acid hydrochloric $(\mathrm{HCl}, 37 \%)$ and sodium perchlorate $\left(\mathrm{NaClO}_{4}\right)$ were purchased from Merck Sdn. Bhd and used without further purification. Aqueous MEA were freshly prepared by dilution of the 99\% MEA with deionized water. All solutions were prepared gravimetrically using analytical balance (Mettler Toledo AS120S) within $\pm 0.0001 \mathrm{~g}$.

\subsection*{2.2. Absorption experimental setup and method}

A new experimental setup that consists of a high pressure stirred tank reactor (STR) coupled with Raman spectroscopy and immersion probe for in situ measurement of $\mathrm{CO}_{2}$ reaction with aqueous MEA solution. STR is made of stainless steel with capacity of 1 L . Schematic diagram of the setup is given in Fig. 1. Pressure was elevated in gas vessel (GV) to supply gas to STR to achieve the desired pressure. Then, 230 mL of aqueous solution of MEA was injected into STR and the solution was degassed by applying vacuum for a short period. After that, $\mathrm{CO}_{2}$ was transferred from GV into STR. Temperature of STR was regulated at the required temperature with oven. Pressures of GV before and after gas transfer were recorded. Experiments were repeated at varying pressures and temperatures.
$\mathrm{CO}_{2}$ loading was determined using conventional gas phase analysis and Raman measurement in liquid phase method developed in this work. $\mathrm{CO}_{2}$ solubility, $\alpha$ ( mol of $\mathrm{CO}_{2} / \mathrm{mol}$ of amine), calculation based on pressure drop as expressed in Eq. (6) is commonly used to compute amount of $\mathrm{CO}_{2}$ absorbed into solvent.

$$
\begin{equation*}
\alpha=\frac{\left[V_{\mathrm{GV}}\left(P_{1} / z_{1}-P_{2} / z_{2}\right)-\left(P_{\text {STR }}\left(V_{\text {STR }}-V_{\text {MEA }}\right) / z\right)\right]}{n_{\text {MEA }, 0} \text { RT }} \tag{6}
\end{equation*}
$$

where $V, P, z, R$ and $n$ are denoted to volume, pressure, compressibility factor, gas constant and number of moles, respectively. 1 and 2 represents condition before and after gas transfer, respectively. Compressibility factors were calculated using Peng Robinson equation of state. The new method introduced in this study determines absorption capacity spectroscopically based on total of carbon containing species detected in liquid phase which represents amount of $\mathrm{CO}_{2}$ absorbed both chemically and physically into aqueous MEA. $\mathrm{CO}_{2}$ loading can be obtained using Eq. (7) with concentrations of carbamate, bicarbonate, carbonate and molecular $\mathrm{CO}_{2}$ in liquid phase predicted with Raman quantitative method discussed in the present paper.

$$
\begin{equation*}
\alpha=\frac{\left(C_{\text {MEACOO }-}+C_{\mathrm{HCO}_{3}}+C_{\mathrm{CO}^{32}}+C_{\mathrm{CO}_{2}}\right.}{C_{\text {MEA }, 0}} \tag{7}
\end{equation*}
$$

where $C$ is concentration of species in mol/kg.

Spectra were collected in the range from 300 to $3100 \mathrm{~cm}^{-1}$. During the initial stages of this study, data collection parameters were varied to determine the best compromise between acquisition time and spectral quality. These trials showed that no significant reduction of noise in spectrum recorded above $40 \mathrm{~s}(\mathrm{~s})$ with 8 accumulations per spectrum. Most observed bands composed of two or more overlapping bands with maxima at slightly different Raman shift. These bands can be mode from different polarized vibrations of amine molecule or other spectroscopically non-equivalent species in the aqueous solutions. The mechanism which Raman bands broadened by superposition of the bands is called inhomogeneous broadening (Pelletier, 2003). To minimize error induced by overlapping bands, standard computer curve-fitting program PeakFit V. 4.12 (Systat Software Inc.) was used to resolve the band envelopes in the region between $300 \mathrm{~cm}^{-1}$ and $3100 \mathrm{~cm}^{-1}$ to locate hidden peaks and fit overlapping peaks to the experimental composite envelope. Band resolution was achieved using deconvolution method, which employs Gaussian response function with a Fourier deconvolution algorithm as given in Eq. (8).

$$
\begin{equation*}
y=2 a_{0}\left[\frac{a_{3} \sqrt{\ln 2}}{a_{2} \sqrt{\pi}} \exp \left(4 \ln 2\left(\frac{x-a_{1}}{a_{2}}\right)^{2}\right)+\frac{1-a_{3}}{\pi a_{2}\left[1+4\left(\frac{x-a_{1}}{a_{2}}\right)^{2}\right]}\right] \tag{8}
\end{equation*}
$$

where, $a_{0}=$ area, $a_{1}=$ center, $a_{2}=$ width ( $>0$ ) and $a_{3}=$ shape. The parameter $\mathrm{a}_{2}$ directly computes the full width at half maximum (FWHM) Lorentzians and Gaussians. The parameter $a_{3}$ varies from 0 to 1 , with 0 being a pure Lorentzian and 1 being a pure Gaussian.

Raman intensity is affected by instrumental settings, such as laser power, monochromator aperture size, spectral resolution and instrument noise, as well as sample factors, such as background scattering, size of sample and properties of sample cell. To eliminate these factors, a ratio of species peak is normalized against another component. For $\mathrm{CO}_{2}$ absorption in solution with amine functional group, sodium perchlorate $\left(\mathrm{NaClO}_{4}\right)$ was commonly used as internal intensity reference (Kim et al., 2008; Wen and Brooker, 1995;

Zhao et al., 2011). $\mathrm{NaClO}_{4}$ (AR grade, Merck Sdn Bhd) was added to all solutions to concentration of $0.517 \mathrm{~mol} / \mathrm{kg}$. Addition of $\mathrm{NaClO}_{4}$ has negligible effect on solubility of $\mathrm{CO}_{2}$ in MEA solution because the salt is not reactive with $\mathrm{CO}_{2}$ and concentration used was low. Preliminary peak search was performed to filter peaks contributed by water and $\mathrm{NaClO}_{4}$ molecules.

Aqueous solution of $\mathrm{NaHCO}_{3}$ and $\mathrm{Na}_{2} \mathrm{CO}_{3}$ with known concentrations were prepared by dissolution of their corresponding sodium salt in deionized water and analyzed by Raman spectroscopy. The highest soluble concentration of $\mathrm{NaHCO}_{3}$ in water is $1 \mathrm{~mol} / \mathrm{kg}$ and $2.2 \mathrm{~mol} / \mathrm{kg}$ for $\mathrm{Na}_{2} \mathrm{CO}_{3}$. For protonated monoethanolamine ( $\mathrm{MEAH}^{+}$) solutions, HCl was added in excess into $99 \% \mathrm{MEA}$ ( 1.1 mol HCl per mole of MEA) to assure MEA is fully converted to $\mathrm{MEAH}^{+}$. HCl was opted because $\mathrm{Cl}^{-}$is not spectroscopically active.

To identify Raman active bands of $\mathrm{MEACOO}{ }^{-}, \mathrm{CO}_{2}$ was added to pure MEA. Without presence of water, formation of $\mathrm{HCO}_{3}{ }^{-}$was suppressed and concentration of dissolved $\mathrm{CO}_{2}$ remained sufficiently low and negligible (Diab et al., 2012). $\mathrm{CO}_{2}$ were fully transformed into carbamate with concomitant protonation of MEA according to the Eq. (9).

$$
\begin{equation*}
2 \mathrm{MEA}+\mathrm{CO}_{2} \leftrightarrow \mathrm{MEACOO}^{-}+\mathrm{MEAH}^{+} \tag{9}
\end{equation*}
$$


Detection of carbamic acid, MEACOOH species as reaction product between $\mathrm{CO}_{2}$ and MEA was precluded in current investigation because MEACOOH is known to be short-lived reaction intermediate. In non aqueous system, MEACOOH is expected to react immediately with MEA, which acts as a base to remove proton from MEACOOH as given in Eq. (10) (Ohno et al., 1999)

$$
\begin{equation*}
\mathrm{MEACOOH}+\mathrm{MEA} \leftrightarrow \mathrm{MEACOO}^{-}+\mathrm{MEAH}^{+} \tag{10}
\end{equation*}
$$


The most apparent evidence for carbamic acid is a $\mathrm{C}=\mathrm{O}$ stretching band of the carboxyl group in the $1650-1740 \mathrm{~cm}^{-1}$ region. In this investigation, no band was observed in the spectral region
expected for carbamic acid. Carbamic acid has not been isolated in spectrometer study so far. (Wen and Brooker, 1995; Yoshida et al., 1989). Therefore, new bands emerged in this carbonated MEA solution were attributed to $\mathrm{MEACOO}{ }^{-}$.

\section*{3. Results and discussion}

\subsection*{3.1. Raman spectral analysis}

MEA has a primary amine functional group of $\mathrm{NH}_{2}$ and a secondary alcohol functional group of OH attached to parent hydrocarbon species of methylene groups as backbone structure. The spectral contributions of amine functional group is characterized by NH bending vibrations which are apparent at $1600 \mathrm{~cm}^{-1}$ in Raman spectrum and CN stretch represented by a weaker band at $1030 \mathrm{~cm}^{-1}$. CO stretching frequency was found at $1100 \mathrm{~cm}^{-1}$. These peaks have weak absorption signal, especially at low concentration and intensity change of these peaks with MEA concentration variation is insignificant which precluded quantitative measurement. For MEA, methylene exhibits the most characteristic group frequencies, which are associated with the CH bend vibration at $1460 \mathrm{~cm}^{-1}$. Strong and broad bands at $2870 \mathrm{~cm}^{-1}$ and $2930 \mathrm{~cm}^{-1}$ are attributed to $\mathrm{CH}_{2}$ symmetric stretch and $\mathrm{CH}_{2}$ asymmetric stretch. Peaks centered at $1460 \mathrm{~cm}^{-1}$, $2870 \mathrm{~cm}^{-1}$, and $2930 \mathrm{~cm}^{-1}$ demonstrate good qualitative measure of MEA concentration. Other relatively weaker bands observed at $1070 \mathrm{~cm}^{-1}, 1240 \mathrm{~cm}^{-1}, 1303 \mathrm{~cm}^{-1}$ and $1352 \mathrm{~cm}^{-1}$ in the spectrum are attributed to CC stretching, $\mathrm{CH}_{2}$ twisting, $\mathrm{CH}_{2}$ twisting and $\mathrm{CH}_{2}$ wagging, respectively (Larkin, 2011). Weak and overlapping peaks of $1259 \mathrm{~cm}^{-1}, 1303 \mathrm{~cm}^{-1}$ and $1352 \mathrm{~cm}^{-1}$ are not distinctive at low concentration, hence are not suitable for quantitative measurement. Raman spectrum of MEA also contains bands at $815 \mathrm{~cm}^{-1}, 845 \mathrm{~cm}^{-1}$ and $873 \mathrm{~cm}^{-1}$, which correspond to those at $819 \mathrm{~cm}^{-1}, 853 \mathrm{~cm}^{-1}$ and $878 \mathrm{~cm}^{-1}$ of ethyl-methylamine, previously assigned to coupled vibrational mode of $\mathrm{CH}_{2}$ rocking and CN stretching (Batista De Carvalho and Teixeira-Dias, 1995). Low frequency bands at $417 \mathrm{~cm}^{-1}$ and $481 \mathrm{~cm}^{-1}$ are contributed by CC deformation (Socrates, 2000). Bands detected at $845 \mathrm{~cm}^{-1}$ and $873 \mathrm{~cm}^{-1}$ are potential candidates to quantify free amine species because of their sufficiently strong band in addition to progressive band growth with increment of amine concentration while weak Raman scattering at $417 \mathrm{~cm}^{-1}, 481 \mathrm{~cm}^{-1}$ and $819 \mathrm{~cm}^{-1}$ were omitted from quantitative study.

Five Raman signals evolved with protonation of MEA with strong acid. Bands located in lower wavenumber region, $1011 \mathrm{~cm}^{-1}$, $1274 \mathrm{~cm}^{-1}$ and $1320 \mathrm{~cm}^{-1}$ were assigned to $\mathrm{CH}_{2}$ twisting, $\mathrm{N}-\mathrm{CH}$ stretch and C-C stretch, correspondingly (Tseng et al., 2010). Stretching vibration of amine hydrohalide containing $\mathrm{NH}_{2}{ }^{+}$was known to result in two strong bands in the region $2700-3000 \mathrm{~cm}^{-1}$, which was observed in this study at $2900 \mathrm{~cm}^{-1}$ and $2975 \mathrm{~cm}^{-1}$ (Socrates, 2000). Intensity of these five Raman bands increase with increasing HCl added into MEA. All bands demonstrated good potential for quantification measurement.

Antisymmetric and symmetric stretching of $\mathrm{COO}^{-}$typically contribute two bands in the $1650-1540 \mathrm{~cm}^{-1}$ and $1450-1360 \mathrm{~cm}^{-1}$ regions in IR spectrum, depending on the molecular constituents (Robinson et al., 2011). These two bands were too weak to be detected in Raman spectrum. MEACOO ${ }^{-}$band had been discovered at $1162 \mathrm{~cm}^{-1}$ in MEA solutions loaded with $\mathrm{CO}_{2}$ though no vibrational mode was assigned (Samarakoon et al., 2013; Souchon et al., 2011). In this work, $\mathrm{CO}_{2}$ absorption into pure MEA gave rise to band at $1155 \mathrm{~cm}^{-1}$, which is identified as carbamate absorbance band due to C-N stretching (Coates, 2006). Quantitative study for MEACOO ${ }^{-}$ions were conducted based on area under $1155 \mathrm{~cm}^{-1}$ peak.

Aqueous $\mathrm{CO}_{2}$ has prominent bands at $1274 \mathrm{~cm}^{-1}$ and $1383 \mathrm{~cm}^{-1}$, which is a Fermi resonance doublet results from a linear combination of fundamental CO2 symmetric stretch and CO2 bend overtone. Both bands detected in present work were shifted to $1278 \mathrm{~cm}^{-1}$ and $1380 \mathrm{~cm}^{-1}$, respectively (Larkin, 2011). Significant growth of band at $1380 \mathrm{~cm}^{-1}$ was observed with increasing $\mathrm{CO}_{2}$ loading indicating that amount of $\mathrm{CO}_{2}$ physically absorbed in reactive solvent is not negligible as remarked by other researchers (Souchon et al., 2011; Wen and Brooker, 1995). Quantification of molecular $\mathrm{CO}_{2}$ in aqueous phase was undertaken with characteristic band centered at $1380 \mathrm{~cm}^{-1}$.

Four fundamental vibrations had been distinguished for $\mathrm{HCO}_{3}{ }^{-}$ ion. Weak bands at $632 \mathrm{~cm}^{-1}, 1360 \mathrm{~cm}^{-1}$ and $1630 \mathrm{~cm}^{-1}$ were attributed to $(\mathrm{OH}) \mathrm{CO}$ bend, symmetric CO stretching and antisymmetric CO stretch, respectively. Sharp and intense band at $1017 \mathrm{~cm}^{-1}$ was contributed by $\mathrm{C}-\mathrm{OH}$ stretching (Davis and Oliver, 1972). Raman spectra obtained in current study captured all four vibrational bands due to $\mathrm{HCO}_{3}{ }^{-}$ion. Area of $1017 \mathrm{~cm}^{-1}$ polarized band was proved to relate linearly with concentration (Wen and Brooker, 1995), hence was selected for quantification measurement in this study.

The isolated $\mathrm{CO}_{3}{ }^{2-}$ ion had been reported to have four Raman active modes of vibration. Intense band at $1065 \mathrm{~cm}^{-1}$ had been assigned to symmetric stretching vibration of $\mathrm{C}-\mathrm{O}$ bond. The doublet structure with bands at $1436 \mathrm{~cm}^{-1}$ and $1380 \mathrm{~cm}^{-1}$ are associated with the antisymmetric stretching of the $\mathrm{C}-\mathrm{O}$ bond of the ion. Band at $680 \mathrm{~cm}^{-1}$ attributed to the antisymmetric deformation of $\mathrm{CO}_{2}$ was not detected in present study (Davis and Oliver, 1972). Strong band at $1065 \mathrm{~cm}^{-1}$ was identified as good candidate for quantitative measurement (Wen and Brooker, 1995). This band demonstrated same property in the present work and was used to quantify concentration of $\mathrm{CO}_{3}{ }^{2-}$ ion in aqueous state.

\subsection*{3.2. Calibration curve}

The intensity of Raman scattered radiation, $I_{\mathrm{r}}$ is given by Eq. (11) (Larkin, 2011).

$$
\begin{equation*}
I_{\mathrm{r}}=v^{4} I_{\mathrm{o}} N(\partial \alpha / \partial Q)^{2} \tag{11}
\end{equation*}
$$

where $v$ is the frequency of exciting laser, $I_{\mathrm{O}}$ is the incident laser intensity, $N$ is the number of scattering molecules, $\alpha$ is the polarizability of the molecule, $Q$ is the vibrational amplitude and ( $\partial \alpha / \partial Q$ ) is the Raman cross section. Raman cross section is a constant unique for a particular material at specific wavelength. Eq. (11) shows that Raman scattering radiation is directly proportional to number of molecules, hence concentration has a linear relationship with Raman intensity. Peak height calibration curve is often more robust to error caused by peak overlap because peak height is often less sensitive to partial band overlapping compared to peak area measurements. However, peak area measurement is more accurate because more photons are measured in determining peak area than in band height (Pelletier, 1999). Peak area method is also more thorough as it takes into account band width and shape changes due to concentration change in solution. Therefore, area under peak was selected as the parameter to construct the calibration curve for determination of species concentration. Based on Eq. (11), concentration of a solution can be linearly related to Raman absorbance intensity when both laser power and frequency of exciting laser are set constant. In this study, relative molar scattering factor, $J$ was introduced to account for all invariable elements that affect Raman intensity measurement. Effect of temperature on Raman spectrum is negligible in the studied temperature range of $30-50{ }^{\circ} \mathrm{C}$ (Azbej et al., 2007; Vogt et al., 2011).

$$

\frac{A_{\mathrm{v}}}{A_{933}}=J_{\mathrm{v}} C_{\mathrm{i}} \tag{12}

\section*{4. Conclusions}

Characteristic peak identified for quantitative Raman study for MEA, protonated MEA, carbamate, bicarbonate, carbonate and dissolved molecular $\mathrm{CO}_{2}$ in aqueous is located at $2930 \mathrm{~cm}^{-1}$, $2975 \mathrm{~cm}^{-1}, 1155 \mathrm{~cm}^{-1}, 1015 \mathrm{~cm}^{-1}, 1066 \mathrm{~cm}^{-1}$ and $1380 \mathrm{~cm}^{-1}$, respectively. A systematic quantitative method for chemical speciation of $\mathrm{CO}_{2}$ loaded amine solution by Raman spectrometer was presented. Results of $\mathrm{CO}_{2}$ loading from in situ Raman measurement was validated with conventional gas pressure drop method from 1 to 60 bar and temperature from 303.15 to 323.15 K. Raman spectroscopic method is proved to be effective to investigate distribution of ions in complex multicomponent reaction system and may provides valuable information for thermodynamic and kinetic modeling of reactive absorption process. In addition to analyzing evolution of reactants and products in equilibrium reactions, current Raman study is able to reveal amount of molecular $\mathrm{CO}_{2}$ physically absorbed in liquid phase. This opens up opportunity of direct measurement of non reacted $\mathrm{CO}_{2}$ content in loaded MEA solution for Henry's constant determination.
