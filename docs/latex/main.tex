\shorttitle{Full-Ionic ePC-SAFT for Aqueous MEA}
\shortauthors{Polley et al.}

\title[mode = title]{Toward a Full-Ionic ePC-SAFT Description of Aqueous Monoethanolamine for \texorpdfstring{\COtwo}{CO2} Capture}

\author[1]{Tanner W. Polley}
\ead{tannerwpolley@gmail.com}

\begin{abstract}
Aqueous monoethanolamine remains a benchmark solvent for post-combustion \COtwo capture, but its process behavior is controlled by a coupled vapor--liquid, chemical-equilibrium, and electrolyte-speciation problem. This work develops a first full-ionic electrolyte PC-SAFT workflow for the loaded \MEA--\HtwoO--\COtwo system using a true-species liquid basis and the advanced ePC-SAFT electrolyte framework with concentration-dependent permittivity and solvation-shell/dielectric-saturation Born contributions. Literature vapor--liquid-equilibrium and NMR speciation data are assembled on a common \COtwo-loading basis, transformed into charge-balanced liquid states containing \COtwo, \MEA, \HtwoO, \MEAH, \MEACOO, \HCOthree, \COthree, \HthreeO, and \Hydroxide, and evaluated through ePC-SAFT activity, fugacity, and reactive bubble-pressure calculations. The workflow reproduces the legacy six-species pressure benchmark, verifies neutral ePC-SAFT parity against the older PC-SAFT pressure path, and produces full-ionic pressure and speciation artifacts for all assembled VLE and speciation records. The current fitted-parameter artifact should be interpreted as a regression-ready seed set rather than a completed final optimum; it nevertheless demonstrates the full thermodynamic coupling required for later parameter estimation. The resulting manuscript framework identifies the literature basis, governing equations, regression targets, and figure set needed for a final predictive ePC-SAFT model of loaded aqueous MEA.
\end{abstract}
