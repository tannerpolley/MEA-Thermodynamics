\section{Conclusion}\label{sec:conclusion}
This work establishes a first-draft scientific workflow for modeling loaded aqueous \MEA with a full-ionic ePC-SAFT formulation.  Literature VLE and NMR speciation data were assembled on a common loading basis, transformed into a charge-balanced true-species liquid representation, and evaluated through ePC-SAFT calculations that include activity-coupled reaction equilibria, electrolyte fugacity calculations, concentration-dependent permittivity, and advanced Born solvation options.

The generated evidence shows that the historical six-species pressure baseline is reproducible, that neutral ePC-SAFT reproduces the corresponding neutral PC-SAFT pressure path, and that the full ionic ePC-SAFT workflow can produce \COtwo pressure and speciation comparisons for the assembled VLE and speciation data sets.  The full ionic evaluation solved all 161 pressure records and all 74 speciation records without recorded state failures.  With the present seed parameter set, the pressure comparison has a median absolute logarithmic residual of approximately 0.36, while the major measured liquid species show substantially smaller deviations than the trace species near the numerical floor.

The principal remaining scientific task is parameter estimation, not workflow construction.  The parameter artifact reported here is a regression-ready seed set rather than a final optimized ePC-SAFT model.  A complete parameter study should fit the selected \MEA, \MEAH, \MEACOO, bicarbonate, and binary-interaction parameters; report active bounds and objective-function improvement; and verify that the final fit improves \COtwo pressure without degrading the measured liquid speciation.  Once that final parameter set is available, the same activity- and fugacity-based workflow can be transferred into absorber--stripper simulations for carbon-capture process analysis.
