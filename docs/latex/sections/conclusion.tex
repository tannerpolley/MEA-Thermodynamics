\section{Conclusions}\label{sec:conclusion}
This work evaluates loaded aqueous \MEA with an explicit true-species thermodynamic basis.  Literature VLE and NMR speciation data were assembled on a common \COtwo-loading basis, transformed into a charge-balanced liquid representation, and evaluated with both an ideal Smith--Missen-style baseline and an activity-coupled ePC-SAFT calculation.  The activity-based calculation includes ePC-SAFT activities and fugacities, concentration-dependent permittivity, and SSM+DS Born solvation terms.

The ideal-equilibrium baseline reproduces the main amine and bicarbonate speciation trends while exposing the temperature dependence of the pressure baseline.  The reported major-species median absolute \(\log_{10}\) residuals are 0.055 for \MEA, 0.044 for \MEAH, 0.029 for \MEACOO, 0.227 for \HCOthree, and 0.0087 for \ce{MEA + MEAH+}.  The 40 and \SI{60}{\degreeCelsius} pressure rows have larger deviations than the higher-temperature rows, so the baseline is retained as a reaction and balance convention check rather than as the final activity-based result.

The activity-coupled ePC-SAFT calculation converged for all 161 pressure records and 74 speciation records.  The pressure residual is 0.493 on the median absolute \(\log_{10}(P_{\mathrm{model}}/P_{\mathrm{data}})\) scale, corresponding to a median factor error of about 3.1.  Nonzero measured-target speciation residuals are 0.074 for \MEA, 0.033 for \MEAH, 0.040 for \MEACOO, 0.461 for \HCOthree, and 0.066 for \ce{MEA + MEAH+}.  Measurements reported as zero and treated as upper-bound targets remain below a modeled \HCOthree mole-fraction upper bound of 0.00128.  Carbonate and trace water-ion quantities remain the limiting species family.

The strongest parameter claim is therefore specific.  \MEAH and \MEACOO have direct \MEA-system speciation evidence, while carbonate-family and water autoionization ions rely on literature-backed values, regularized sensitivity checks, and charge-balance constraints.  The present work does not report a completed coupled pressure/speciation regression.  The reported parameter set is therefore evaluated as a fixed activity-based parameter set with explicit residuals.  It should not be used as a standalone process-design pressure correlation without additional pressure/speciation regression and validation evidence.
