\section{Conclusion}\label{sec:conclusion}
This work evaluates loaded aqueous \MEA with an explicit true-species thermodynamic basis.  Literature VLE and NMR speciation data were assembled on a common \COtwo-loading basis, transformed into a charge-balanced liquid representation, and evaluated with both an ideal Smith--Missen-style baseline and an activity-coupled ePC-SAFT calculation.  The activity-based calculation includes ePC-SAFT activities and fugacities, concentration-dependent permittivity, and SSM+DS Born solvation terms.

The ideal-equilibrium baseline solves the five-reaction, nine-species system and reproduces the major observed amine and bicarbonate speciation trends.  Its accepted major-species median absolute $\log_{10}$ residuals are 0.055 for \MEA, 0.044 for \MEAH, 0.029 for \MEACOO, 0.227 for \HCOthree, and 0.0087 for \ce{MEA + MEAH+}.  The pressure baseline remains temperature dependent, with accepted high-temperature rows but larger 40 and \SI{60}{\degreeCelsius} deviations.

The activity-coupled ePC-SAFT calculation solves all 161 pressure records and all 74 speciation records.  The residual-qualified pressure metric is 0.493 on the median absolute $\log_{10}(P_{\mathrm{model}}/P_{\mathrm{data}})$ scale.  Accepted direct-positive speciation metrics are 0.074 for \MEA, 0.033 for \MEAH, 0.040 for \MEACOO, 0.461 for \HCOthree, and 0.066 for \ce{MEA + MEAH+}.  Reported-zero bicarbonate rows remain below a modeled mole-fraction upper bound of 0.00128.  Carbonate and trace water-ion quantities remain the limiting species family.

The strongest parameter claim is therefore specific.  \MEAH and \MEACOO have direct \MEA-system speciation evidence, while carbonate-family and water autoionization ions rely on literature-backed values, regularized sensitivity checks, and charge-balance constraints.  A completed coupled pressure/speciation regression is still required before the model can be described as a pressure-optimized global parameterization.  Future work should couple pressure, direct-positive speciation, reported-zero upper bounds, active-bound reporting, and sensitivity analysis in one regression calculation while preserving the accepted amine-family speciation behavior reported here.
