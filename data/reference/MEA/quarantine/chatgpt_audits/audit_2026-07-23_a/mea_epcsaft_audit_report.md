# Primary-source thermodynamics data audit: reactive MEA–H₂O–CO₂ ePC-SAFT
**Audit date:** 2026-07-23  
**Scope:** CO₂, MEA, H₂O, MEAH⁺, MEACOO⁻, HCO₃⁻, CO₃²⁻, H₃O⁺, OH⁻; byproducts retained separately.  
**No parameter regression, model fitting, interpolation, or silent basis conversion was performed.**

**Search-completeness caveat:** this is a best-effort audit of web-accessible primary sources and reference chains, not proof that no uncatalogued, paywalled, archival, or author-held dataset exists. Negative findings are therefore recorded as `not found in the audited search`, not as universal nonexistence claims.
## Strict provenance verdict
**No source in this bundle is yet source-complete under the requested standard.** The browser research environment exposed readable primary-paper pages, tables, and repository/mirror renderings, but did not expose the exact remote PDF bytes to the local filesystem. Consequently, every `source_file_sha256` and `local_source_path` field is blank, every row is `source_complete=no`, and every numerical row is `regression_ready=no`. The extracted measurements are therefore a **quarantined acquisition ledger**, not a regression dataset.
The audit contains **64 source/negative-source records**: **8 found with extracted evidence**, **52 found but presently unusable**, and **4 explicit not-found blockers**. It contains **608 unconverted row records** from eight sources.

### Classification rules applied
- `direct measurement`: apparatus output or directly assayed quantity.
- `aggregate measurement`: a directly measured unresolved sum, such as MEA + MEAH⁺.
- `calibration-derived`: obtained through a traceable calibration/correction chain, such as Jou pCO₂.
- `balance-inferred`: reconstructed from material/charge/alkalinity balances; not independent species evidence.
- `model-derived` or `fitted`: excluded from experimental rows.
Every extracted row retains a `leakage_group`. Recommended splits are by complete source, apparatus, concentration, temperature campaign, sequence, or prepared stock—not by random rows.

## 6. Recommended training/validation split and leakage controls
1. **Never random-split rows from one source.** Group by DOI/source, lab, apparatus, calibration, prepared stock, loading sequence, and property campaign.
2. **Binary VLE:** train on complete pressure/source blocks and hold out an independent still/source. Kim repeated 60 °C sequences must remain grouped.
3. **CO₂ VLE:** hold out entire concentration and source campaigns. A defensible example after provenance closure is train on selected Jou temperature blocks plus one Wagner concentration, then validate on the other Wagner concentration/source block. Do not mix Jou GC/BaCO₃ duplicate assays as independent samples.
4. **Speciation:** hold out whole NMR/Raman/titration campaigns and their calibrations. Compare model predictions only at the source’s resolution—for example, MEA + MEAH⁺, not separate species.
5. **Dielectric:** keep each full spectrum and all parameters fitted from it together. A fitted εs, conductivity, relaxation times, and strengths share covariance.
6. **Physical/calorimetric:** group all temperatures measured on one prepared solution. Density and viscosity from the same stock are correlated. Preserve each heat definition and sign as a separate observable.
7. **Secondary encodings and fits:** ThermoML, empirical coefficients, model curves, balance-inferred species, and review reproductions must never enter a validation set as independent evidence.
## 7. Unresolved blockers
1. Acquire the exact publisher or authoritative repository file for every source, save it locally, and compute SHA-256. Until then, all 608 rows remain quarantined.
2. Visually verify all transcriptions against those exact files, especially Park–Lee OCR and the Kim `59.11 °C` entry; retain an audit trail for any correction.
3. Recover Aronu et al. 2011 tables/SI with unambiguous separation of experimental pCO₂ from extended-UNIQUAC predictions; this is the key 15/30/45/60 mass-% source.
4. Locate a source-complete exact 20 mass-% MEA CO₂-equilibrium/VLE campaign, or commission one. Nearby concentrations and normality data are not substitutes.
5. Acquire quantitative NMR/Raman/FTIR/titration tables and calibration files. Matin Table S1/S2 and Böttinger/Jakobsen numerical species rows are high priority.
6. Find or measure independent species evidence that separates free MEA from MEAH⁺ and HCO₃⁻ from CO₃²⁻; otherwise validation must remain at aggregate resolution.
7. Find or measure true static/near-DC ε₀ for CO₂-loaded MEA with conductivity, electrode-polarization handling, extrapolation equation, uncertainty, and covariance.
8. Find or measure direct MEAH⁺/MEACOO⁻ target-ion activity/osmotic/EMF evidence. pKa and analog salts do not close this gap.
9. Find or measure equilibrium pH with complete scale, calibration standards, electrode method, ionic-strength treatment, T/P/composition/loading, equilibration proof, and uncertainty.
10. Acquire primary density/viscosity/N₂O/CO₂-physical-solubility tables from Amundsen, Hartono, Han, Versteeg, and related sources; cross-check ThermoML as a secondary encoding.
11. Acquire raw excess-enthalpy and CO₂-absorption calorimetry tables with differential/integral/semi-differential definition, reference state, sign convention, calibration, and covariance.
12. Acquire quantitative equilibrium byproduct yields/detection limits. The 2024 HEIA/HEEDA/OZD data qualify physical effects of prepared mixtures, not equilibrium formation rates or yields.
13. Recover pure-MEA density, Cp, vapor-pressure comparison data, and exact broadband dielectric association tables to qualify neutral MEA parameters independently.
