\section{Theory}\label{sec:theory}
The \COtwo-solubility modeling target is a reacting electrolyte vapor--liquid equilibrium problem.  At specified temperature, apparent amine concentration, and total absorbed \COtwo loading, the model must determine a charge-balanced true-species liquid composition and the corresponding vapor-phase \COtwo partial pressure.  The liquid composition enters the EOS derivatives directly through ePC-SAFT fugacity coefficients, activity coefficients, and Born/electrostatic contributions.

\subsection{PC-SAFT residual Helmholtz energy}
ePC-SAFT is formulated as a residual Helmholtz-energy equation of state.  For a state defined by temperature \(T\), molar density \(\rho\), and mole-fraction vector \(\boldsymbol{x}\), the reduced residual Helmholtz energy \(\tilde{a}^{\mathrm{res}}=a^{\mathrm{res}}/(RT)\) is assembled from hard-chain, dispersion, association, Debye--Huckel, and Born terms \cite{Gross2001,Gross2002,Cameretti2005,Bulow2021a,Figiel2025}:
\begin{equation}
    \tilde{a}^{\mathrm{res}}
    =
    \tilde{a}^{\mathrm{hc}}
    +\tilde{a}^{\mathrm{disp}}
    +\tilde{a}^{\mathrm{assoc}}
    +\tilde{a}^{\mathrm{DH}}
    +\tilde{a}^{\mathrm{Born}} ,
    \label{eq:epcsaft-residual-split}
\end{equation}
where the hard-chain contribution gives the repulsive chain reference, the dispersion contribution gives short-range attraction, the association contribution gives site-specific hydrogen bonding, the Debye--Huckel term gives long-range electrostatics, and the Born term gives electrostatic solvation.  This split is central for loaded \MEA because neutral solvent structure, ionic strength, and solvation all change with \COtwo loading.  Neutral \MEA and \HtwoO therefore require segment, dispersion, and association parameters, whereas charged species also require charge, dielectric, and Born-diameter information.

The hard-chain reference is built from packing-fraction moments
\begin{equation}
    \zeta_n=\frac{\pi}{6}\rho\sum_i x_i m_i d_i^n,\qquad n\in\{0,1,2,3\},
    \label{eq:epcsaft-zeta-moments}
\end{equation}
where \(m_i\) is the segment number and \(d_i\) is the temperature-dependent segment diameter.  With \(\bar{m}=\sum_i x_i m_i\), the chain correction subtracts the connectivity penalty from the hard-sphere reference,
\begin{equation}
    \tilde{a}^{\mathrm{hc}}
    =
    \bar{m}\tilde{a}^{\mathrm{hs}}
    -
    \sum_i x_i(m_i-1)\ln g_{ii}^{\mathrm{hs}}(\sigma_{ii}) .
    \label{eq:epcsaft-hard-chain}
\end{equation}
This term controls excluded volume and the density scale used by all subsequent residual derivatives, so it influences both fugacity coefficients and liquid-state activity coefficients.

The dispersion term introduces attractive perturbations through mixture moments of segment energy and size,
\begin{equation}
    \tilde{a}^{\mathrm{disp}}
    =
    -2\pi\rho I_1(\eta,\bar{m})\overline{m^2\epsilon\sigma^3}
    -\pi\rho\bar{m}C_1 I_2(\eta,\bar{m})
    \overline{m^2\epsilon^2\sigma^3},
    \label{eq:epcsaft-dispersion}
\end{equation}
where \(\eta=\zeta_3\), \(I_1\) and \(I_2\) are the PC-SAFT dispersion integrals, and the overbars denote the corresponding pair-mixed composition averages.  Binary interaction parameters enter this contribution through the mixed dispersion energy \(\epsilon_{ij}\), so pressure residuals can be sensitive to neutral--neutral and ion--molecule dispersion choices.

Association is represented through site fractions \(X^{A_i}\),
\begin{equation}
    \tilde{a}^{\mathrm{assoc}}
    =
    \sum_i x_i \sum_{A_i}
    \left[
        \ln X^{A_i}
        -\frac{X^{A_i}}{2}
        +\frac{1}{2}
    \right],
    \label{eq:epcsaft-association}
\end{equation}
with association strengths proportional to
\begin{equation}
    \Delta^{A_iB_j}_{ij}
    =
    d_{ij}^{3}g_{ij}^{\mathrm{hs}}
    \kappa^{A_iB_j}_{ij}
    \left[
        \exp\left(\frac{\epsilon^{A_iB_j}_{ij}}{k_{\mathrm{B}}T}\right)-1
    \right].
    \label{eq:epcsaft-association-strength}
\end{equation}
This contribution carries the hydrogen-bonding physics of water and alkanolamines; ions without assigned association sites do not contribute association-site balances in this formulation.

\subsection{Electrolyte PC-SAFT and ion activity coefficients}
For charged species, the Debye--Huckel contribution uses the dielectric state and the inverse Debye length,
\begin{align}
    \kappa
    &=
    \left[
        \frac{\rho e^2}{k_{\mathrm{B}}T\varepsilon_0\varepsilon_r}
        \sum_j x_j z_j^2
    \right]^{1/2},
    \label{eq:epcsaft-debye-kappa} \\
    \tilde{a}^{\mathrm{DH}}
    &=
    -\frac{\kappa e^2}{12\pi\varepsilon_0\varepsilon_r k_{\mathrm{B}}T}
    \sum_i x_i z_i^2\chi_i ,
    \label{eq:epcsaft-debye-helmholtz}
\end{align}
where \(z_i\) is the ionic charge and \(\chi_i\) is the finite-ion-size correction.  The same relative permittivity \(\varepsilon_r\) also enters the Born term, making the dielectric rule a coupled electrostatic input rather than a separate post-processing correction.  These electrolyte residual terms enter the residual chemical potentials used to compute ePC-SAFT activity coefficients for the liquid reaction equations.

\subsection{Modified Born term with solvation shell and dielectric saturation}
The Born contribution is evaluated in modular form:
\begin{equation}
    \tilde{a}^{\mathrm{Born}}
    =
    -\frac{e^2}{4\pi\varepsilon_0 k_{\mathrm{B}}T}
    \sum_i x_i z_i^2
    \sum_{\delta\in\mathcal{D}}
    \left(1-\frac{1}{\varepsilon_{r,m_\delta}}\right)
    D_{i,\delta}^{(m_\delta)} .
    \label{eq:epcsaft-born-modular}
\end{equation}
The active set \(\mathcal{D}\) always contains the base Born term and may also include the solvation-shell-modeling (SSM) and dielectric-saturation (DS) corrections,
\begin{equation}
    \mathcal{D}
    =
    \{\mathrm{Born}\}\cup\mathcal{D}_{\mathrm{add}},
    \qquad
    \mathcal{D}_{\mathrm{add}}\subseteq\{\mathrm{SSM},\mathrm{DS}\}.
    \label{eq:epcsaft-born-active-set}
\end{equation}
For the solvation-shell modeling plus dielectric-saturation (SSM+DS) option used in this work, \(\mathcal{D}_{\mathrm{add}}=\{\mathrm{SSM},\mathrm{DS}\}\).  The base Born and SSM terms use the bulk dielectric medium, while the DS term uses the ion-local dielectric medium:
\begin{equation}
    m_\delta=
    \begin{cases}
        \mathrm{bulk}, & \delta\in\{\mathrm{Born},\mathrm{SSM}\},\\
        \mathrm{ion}, & \delta=\mathrm{DS}.
    \end{cases}
    \label{eq:epcsaft-born-medium}
\end{equation}
The inverse-diameter terms are
\begin{align}
    D_{i,\mathrm{Born}}^{(\mathrm{bulk})}
    &=
    \frac{1}{d_i^{\mathrm{Born}}},
    \label{eq:epcsaft-born-base-dterm}\\
    D_{i,\mathrm{SSM}}^{(\mathrm{bulk})}
    &=
    \frac{1}{d_i^{\mathrm{Born}}+\Delta d_i}
    -
    \frac{1}{d_i^{\mathrm{Born}}},
    \label{eq:epcsaft-born-ssm-dterm}\\
    D_{i,\mathrm{DS}}^{(\mathrm{ion})}
    &=
    \frac{1}{d_i^{\mathrm{Born}}}
    -
    \frac{1}{d_i^{\mathrm{Born}}+\Delta d_i},
    \label{eq:epcsaft-born-ds-dterm}
\end{align}
with shell shift
\begin{equation}
    \Delta d_i
    =
    \frac{f_{\mathrm{mix}}-1}{|z_i|}\,d_i^{\mathrm{Born}},
    \qquad
    f_{\mathrm{mix}}=\sum_k x_k f_k .
    \label{eq:epcsaft-born-shell-shift}
\end{equation}
The SSM term changes the effective Born radius used with the bulk medium, whereas the DS term transfers the complementary radius increment into the ion-local dielectric medium.  This split is important for loaded \MEA because carbonation changes both the ionic population and the solvent environment.

The ePC-SAFT calculations in this work use concentration-dependent permittivity and modified Born solvation.  The selected modified-Born convention combines an ion-specific Born diameter with solvation-shell modeling, dielectric saturation, and composition-dependent Born chemical-potential corrections.  This is the SSM+DS form introduced for the modified Born contribution, in which an ion-specific Born diameter is combined with a solvent-shell correction and a radially simplified dielectric-saturation term \cite{Figiel2025}.  The corresponding ePC-SAFT literature treats the pure-ion relative permittivity as $\varepsilon_r=8$, sets $f_k=1$ for ions, and fits the solvent $f_k$ parameter to electrolyte activity data; water, methanol, and ethanol have reported $f_k$ values of 1.5, 1.4, and 1.6, respectively \cite{Figiel2025}.  Loaded 30 wt\% \MEA also has direct dielectric-dispersion measurements, which show that static permittivity changes with both temperature and carbonation ratio \cite{Hajj2024}.  Those data motivate, but do not yet fully identify, an MEA-specific solvent-shell parameter.

In physical terms, the liquid dielectric environment is not treated as a fixed pure-water constant.  Instead, the ion solvation contribution responds to solvent composition and ionic strength, which is essential for a loaded amine solution whose polarity, carbamate concentration, and carbonate-family ion population change strongly with \COtwo loading.  The parameter table in \cref{tab:full-ionic-ssm-ds-parameters} therefore distinguishes fitted \MEA-family ion parameters from SSM+DS conventions transferred from electrolyte literature.

\subsection{MEA--CO2--H2O reaction network}
The apparent feed is defined by the unloaded \MEA mass fraction $w_{\MEA}$ and \COtwo loading $\alpha$, with $\alpha$ equal to moles of absorbed \COtwo per mole of total amine.  The unloaded apparent liquid composition is computed from molecular-weight conversion as
\begin{equation}
    x_{\MEA,0}
    =
    \frac{w_{\MEA}}
    {M_{\MEA}/M_{\HtwoO}+w_{\MEA}\left(1-M_{\MEA}/M_{\HtwoO}\right)},
    \qquad
    x_{\HtwoO,0}=1-x_{\MEA,0}.
    \label{eq:unloaded-mea-composition}
\end{equation}
Absorbed \COtwo is then added according to \(n_{\COtwo,0}=\alpha n_{\MEA,0}\), and the total apparent state is normalized before the reaction solve.

The true-species liquid basis resolves the apparent \MEA and \COtwo inventory into molecular and ionic species needed for acid--base and solubility calculations:
\begin{equation}
    \boldsymbol{x}
    =
    \left(
    x_{\COtwo},x_{\MEA},x_{\HtwoO},x_{\MEAH},x_{\MEACOO},
    x_{\HCOthree},x_{\COthree},x_{\HthreeO},x_{\Hydroxide}
    \right).
    \label{eq:true-species-vector}
\end{equation}
The fixed speciation calculation uses the five independent liquid reactions listed in \cref{tab:reaction-equilibrium-constants}.  For reaction $r$, thermodynamic equilibrium is written with activities rather than bare mole fractions,
\begin{equation}
    \sum_i \nu_{ri}\ln a_i = \ln K_r(T),
    \qquad
    a_i = \gamma_i^{\mathrm{ePC-SAFT}} x_i,
    \label{eq:activity-reaction-equilibrium}
\end{equation}
where $\nu_{ri}$ is the stoichiometric coefficient and $\gamma_i^{\mathrm{ePC-SAFT}}$ is the activity coefficient evaluated from the ePC-SAFT liquid state.  The reaction constants are represented as
\begin{equation}
    \ln K_r(T)=A_r+\frac{B_r}{T}+C_r\ln T+D_rT,
    \label{eq:reaction-equilibrium-constant-temperature}
\end{equation}
with \(T\) in kelvin and a mole-fraction activity convention.  The reported temperature ranges in \cref{tab:reaction-equilibrium-constants} are the source ranges for the coefficient correlations; rows outside a listed range are retained as fixed-basis residual tests rather than as refitted reaction-constant evidence.  This activity-coupled form is the key distinction from the ideal-equilibrium baseline.

The liquid composition also satisfies carbon, amine, and water pseudo-component balances plus electroneutrality:
\begin{align}
    x_{\COtwo,0} &= x_{\COtwo}+x_{\MEACOO}+x_{\HCOthree}+x_{\COthree}, \\
    x_{\MEA,0} &= x_{\MEA}+x_{\MEAH}+x_{\MEACOO}, \\
    x_{\HtwoO,0} &= x_{\HtwoO}+x_{\HCOthree}+x_{\COthree}+x_{\HthreeO}+x_{\Hydroxide}, \\
    x_{\MEAH}+x_{\HthreeO} &= x_{\MEACOO}+x_{\HCOthree}+2x_{\COthree}+x_{\Hydroxide}.
    \label{eq:true-species-balances}
\end{align}

\subsection{Phase-equilibrium conditions and reference states}
For a liquid state at temperature \(T\), pressure \(P\), and mole-fraction vector \(\boldsymbol{x}\), ePC-SAFT provides residual chemical potentials and fugacity coefficients.  Molecular vapor--liquid equilibrium is expressed through fugacity equality,
\begin{equation}
    f_i^{\liq}(T,P,\boldsymbol{x}^{\liq})
    =
    f_i^{\vap}(T,P,\boldsymbol{y}^{\vap}),
    \qquad i \in \{\COtwo,\HtwoO,\MEA\},
    \label{eq:fugacity-equality}
\end{equation}
while ionic species are constrained to the liquid phase.  The residual Helmholtz contribution connects the phase-equilibrium calculation to the activity coefficients used in the reaction equations through
\begin{equation}
    \ln\varphi_k
    =
    \tilde{\mu}_k^{\mathrm{res}}-\ln Z,
    \qquad
    \ln\gamma_i^{*}
    =
    \ln\varphi_i-\ln\varphi_i^{\infty},
    \label{eq:epcsaft-phi-gamma-link}
\end{equation}
where \(\varphi_i^{\infty}\) is the corresponding infinite-dilution reference fugacity coefficient used for the liquid-phase activity coefficient.  This reference-state convention keeps neutral and ionic liquid activities on the same true-species reaction basis.  Each residual contribution therefore affects both phase equilibrium and the activity coefficients used in the reaction equations.
