\section{ePC-SAFT Equation of State}

Electrolyte PC-SAFT (ePC-SAFT) is an extension of the PC-SAFT equation of state designed to handle systems containing ions and electrolytes by incorporating long-range electrostatic interactions. The original ePC-SAFT formulation was proposed by Cameretti et al.\ in 2005~\cite{Cameretti2005}. In ePC-SAFT, the standard PC-SAFT terms (hard-chain, dispersion, association) are supplemented with an additional term (often based on the Debye-Hückel theory) to account for the electrostatic contributions of charged species in solution~\cite{Cameretti2005}. This allows the equation of state to describe systems where dissociation or chemical reactions produce ions, as is the case for $\COtwo$ reacting with amines.

In the context of amine solutions, ePC-SAFT treats species such as $\MEAH$ (protonated $\MEA$), $\MEACOO$ (carbamate), $\HCOthree$, and other ions as distinct components in equilibrium with neutral molecules. The model requires parameters for these ionic species as well as cross-interaction parameters between ions and neutral molecules. Because ions carry charge and strongly interact with polar solvents like water, ePC-SAFT also introduces a dielectric continuum (to apply Debye-Hückel theory for coulombic interactions). The 2005 implementation by Cameretti et al.\ demonstrated that ePC-SAFT could correlate activity coefficients and osmotic coefficients in aqueous electrolyte solutions~\cite{Cameretti2005}.
