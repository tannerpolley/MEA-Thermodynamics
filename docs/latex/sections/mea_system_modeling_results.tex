\section{Results and Discussion}\label{sec:mea-system-modeling-results}
This section reports the data basis, baseline equilibrium calculation, true-species ePC-SAFT activity evaluation, parameter evidence, and remaining regression limits for the \MEA--\COtwo--\HtwoO system.

\subsection{Experimental Data Basis}
The VLE data set contains 161 \COtwo partial-pressure records from Aronu et al., Hilliard, Jou et al., Mamun et al., and Xu et al.\cite{Aronu2011,Hilliard2008,Jou1995,Mamun2005,Xu2011}.  Each record contains amine mass fraction, temperature, \COtwo loading, and measured \COtwo pressure.  The speciation data set contains 74 NMR-derived liquid-composition records from Boettinger et al., Jakobsen et al., and Matin et al.\cite{Bottinger2008,Jakobsen2005,Matin2012}.  The speciation records are expressed on a common loading basis and reconciled to satisfy carbon, amine, water, and charge balances before model comparison.

\subsection{Ideal Equilibrium Baseline}
The ideal Smith--Missen-style baseline solves the same five-reaction, nine-species liquid equilibrium problem with activities set equal to mole fractions.  It provides a convention check for the reaction set before ePC-SAFT activity coefficients are introduced.  For major observed species, the overall median absolute $\log_{10}(\mathrm{model}/\mathrm{data})$ residuals are 0.055 for \MEA, 0.044 for \MEAH, 0.029 for \MEACOO, 0.227 for \HCOthree, and 0.0087 for the aggregate \ce{MEA + MEAH+}.  Molecular \COtwo and \COthree are solved and plotted, but their residual rows are not used as major-species validation evidence.

The corresponding pressure baseline gives an overall median absolute $\log_{10}$ pressure residual of 0.160.  The temperature-resolved pressure comparison is uneven: the 40 and \SI{60}{\degreeCelsius} rows do not meet the pressure residual criterion, whereas the 80, 100, and \SI{120}{\degreeCelsius} rows do.  The baseline is therefore used as a reproducibility and convention check, not as the final activity-based thermodynamic result.

\subsection{ePC-SAFT Parameter Set}
The activity-based calculation uses a nine-species liquid basis: \COtwo, \MEA, \HtwoO, \MEAH, \MEACOO, \HCOthree, \COthree, \HthreeO, and \Hydroxide.  The parameter set in \cref{tab:full-ionic-ssm-ds-parameters} combines inherited neutral molecular parameters, SSM+DS ion conventions from modified-Born ePC-SAFT literature, and direct \MEA-system regression for the two amine-family ions best supported by the NMR/speciation data.  The parameter-evidence matrix in \cref{tab:parameter-evidence-matrix} separates directly fitted parameters from literature-backed trace-ion quantities.
The temperature-dependent water segment diameter used in \cref{tab:full-ionic-ssm-ds-parameters} is
\begin{equation}
    \sigma_{\HtwoO}(T)=2.7927+10.11\exp(-0.01775T)-1.417\exp(-0.01146T),
    \label{eq:water-sigma-temperature}
\end{equation}
where \(T\) is in kelvin and \(\sigma_{\HtwoO}\) is in angstroms.  Rows marked ``This work'' contain parameters fitted or derived in the present analysis; cited rows use literature values.

The binary-interaction matrix is intentionally sparse.  It retains the inherited molecular term and the directly fitted amine-ion term,
\begin{align*}
k_{\MEA,\HtwoO} &= -0.052, &
k_{\MEAH,\MEACOO} &= -0.002018,
\end{align*}
and uses literature water--ion interactions for the proton/carbonate family,
\begin{align*}
k_{\HtwoO,\HthreeO} &= 0.25, &
k_{\HtwoO,\Hydroxide} &= -0.25,\\
k_{\HtwoO,\HCOthree} &= 0, &
k_{\HtwoO,\COthree} &= -0.25.
\end{align*}
The remaining \MEA-specific ion--molecule and ion--ion terms are held at zero for this evaluation.  This sparse interaction structure tests the selected activity model before introducing a jointly optimized binary-interaction matrix.

\subsection{Activity-Coupled ePC-SAFT Evaluation}
The true-species ePC-SAFT activity calculation solves all 161 assembled VLE records and all 74 speciation records.  The accepted pressure metric is a median absolute $\log_{10}(P_{\mathrm{model}}/P_{\mathrm{data}})$ residual of 0.493 across the pressure data.  Temperature-resolved pressure errors remain largest between 40 and \SI{80}{\degreeCelsius} and decrease at \SI{120}{\degreeCelsius}.  This result establishes an activity-based pressure evaluation with quantified residuals; it does not establish a pressure-optimized global parameter set.

The accepted direct-positive speciation metrics are 0.074 for \MEA, 0.033 for \MEAH, 0.040 for \MEACOO, 0.461 for \HCOthree, and 0.066 for the aggregate \ce{MEA + MEAH+}, all on the median absolute $\log_{10}(\mathrm{model}/\mathrm{data})$ scale.  Reported-zero \HCOthree rows are treated as upper-bound targets; the maximum modeled mole fraction for those rows is 0.00128.  Carbonate direct-positive rows have a larger median residual of 0.599 and are not used as accepted carbonate validation evidence.

\subsection{Direct Amine-Ion Regression Evidence}
The direct amine-ion regression uses Tier A speciation targets from Matin et al., Jakobsen et al., and Boettinger et al.\cite{Matin2012,Jakobsen2005,Bottinger2008}.  The fitted variables are $\sigma$, $\epsilon/k$, and $d^{\mathrm{Born}}$ for \MEAH and \MEACOO, plus $k_{\MEAH,\MEACOO}$.  The fitted subset decreases the residual norm from 0.2714 to 0.2677 and changes the median absolute $\log_{10}(\mathrm{model}/\mathrm{data})$ error from 0.1010 to 0.0988.  The fitted \MEAH $R^2$ changes from 0.655 to 0.665, and the fitted \MEACOO $R^2$ changes from 0.888 to 0.905.  No retained fit parameter is at a bound.

\subsection{Trace-Ion Sensitivity and Identifiability}
\HCOthree and \COthree are observed in the speciation data, but their parameter movement is less stable than the amine-family ions.  The regularized trace-carbonate fit retains $d_{\HCOthree}^{\mathrm{Born}}=3.0$ and $d_{\COthree}^{\mathrm{Born}}=3.0$, with a residual norm of 0.702.  A separate unanchored multistart check finds a lower residual near $d_{\HCOthree}^{\mathrm{Born}}=6.80$ and $d_{\COthree}^{\mathrm{Born}}=3.00$, decreasing the trace-carbonate residual norm to 0.683.  That alternative is not used in the reported activity-based evaluation because it is fitted only to carbonate-family residuals and is not coupled to the pressure/speciation objective.  \HthreeO and \Hydroxide are controlled mainly by charge balance and water autoionization and are not independently identifiable from the available NMR targets.

\subsection{Coupled Regression Status and Transferability}
The all-row coupled pressure/speciation target set contains all 161 pressure rows and all 74 speciation rows.  The present work does not report a completed coupled regression because the available ePC-SAFT regression method does not provide the sensitivity information required to accept a pressure/speciation fit over this target set.  The selected parameter set is therefore evaluated as a fixed activity-based parameter set with residual-qualified pressure and speciation claims.

The deterministic split analysis uses Aronu, Hilliard, Idris, and Mamun as pressure-training families with Jou and Xu held out for pressure validation; Matin and Boettinger are used for speciation training with Jakobsen held out for validation.  The held-out results show stronger transfer for directly fitted amine-family species than for carbonate-family species.  A reduced finite-difference sensitivity screen identifies $k_{\MEA,\HtwoO}$, $k_{\COtwo,\MEA}$, and $d_{\COthree}^{\mathrm{Born}}$ as the strongest tracked directions in the reported objective neighborhood, while $d_{\HthreeO}^{\mathrm{Born}}$ remains near-zero on the reported metrics.  These results indicate that further pressure-weighted fitting should move binary interaction parameters and carbonate Born terms only under a coupled objective that preserves the accepted amine-speciation behavior.
