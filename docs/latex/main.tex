\shorttitle{SSM+DS ePC-SAFT Modeling of CO2 Solubility in Aqueous MEA}
\shortauthors{Polley}

\title[mode = title]{\texorpdfstring{SSM+DS Modified-Born ePC-SAFT Modeling of \ce{CO2} Solubility in Aqueous Monoethanolamine with Speciation Constraints}{SSM+DS Modified-Born ePC-SAFT Modeling of CO2 Solubility in Aqueous Monoethanolamine with Speciation Constraints}}

\author{Tanner W. Polley}
\ead{tannerwpolley@gmail.com}

\begin{abstract}
Carbon dioxide (\COtwo) solubility in aqueous monoethanolamine (\MEA) is usually reported through the equilibrium \COtwo partial pressure at fixed solvent loading, but the pressure depends on the ionic liquid composition formed by carbamate and carbonate reactions.  We extend solvation-shell modeling plus dielectric-saturation (SSM+DS) modified-Born electrolyte perturbed-chain statistical associating fluid theory (ePC-SAFT) to loaded \MEA--\HtwoO--\COtwo using a true-species liquid basis containing \COtwo, \MEA, \HtwoO, \MEAH, \MEACOO, \HCOthree, \COthree, \HthreeO, and \Hydroxide.  Literature vapor--liquid-equilibrium (VLE) solubility records and nuclear magnetic resonance (NMR) speciation data were converted to a common loading basis over 30 wt\% \MEA, 20--\SI{120}{\degreeCelsius}, and \COtwo loading 0.003--0.990.  An ideal Smith--Missen five-reaction baseline reproduced major amine-speciation trends, with median absolute \(\log_{10}(\mathrm{model}/\mathrm{data})\) residuals of 0.055 for \MEA, 0.044 for \MEAH, 0.029 for \MEACOO, and 0.227 for \HCOthree.  The same reaction network was then evaluated with ePC-SAFT activities, fugacities, concentration-dependent permittivity, and SSM+DS Born terms.  On the 31-record intersection, median absolute pressure residuals were 0.160 for the ideal baseline and 0.495 for the activity model, so the present fixed activity treatment did not improve pressure accuracy.  The activity calculation nevertheless converged for all 161 solubility-pressure records, for which its contextual median residual was 0.493, and for 74 speciation records; direct-positive speciation residuals were 0.074 for \MEA, 0.033 for \MEAH, 0.040 for \MEACOO, and 0.461 for \HCOthree.  The fixed parameter set provides a residual-qualified advanced-Born ePC-SAFT test for \COtwo solubility in loaded \MEA; carbonate-family Born identifiability remains the main limitation.
\end{abstract}
