\section{Results and Discussion}\label{sec:mea-system-modeling-results}
The main result is that the ideal baseline verifies the reaction and balance conventions, and the activity-coupled ePC-SAFT calculation converges on the assembled pressure and speciation records with explicit residuals.  \MEA, \MEAH, and \MEACOO are the strongest supported liquid observables.  Bicarbonate, carbonate, and trace water ions remain limiting observables, and the pressure residuals are reported as a fixed activity-based evaluation rather than a pressure-optimized model.

\subsection{Ideal Baseline Convention Check}
The ideal Smith--Missen-style baseline uses the same five-reaction, nine-species liquid equilibrium problem with activities set equal to mole fractions.  It provides a convention check for the reaction set before ePC-SAFT activity coefficients are introduced.  For major observed species, the overall median absolute $\log_{10}(\mathrm{model}/\mathrm{data})$ residuals are 0.055 for \MEA, 0.044 for \MEAH, 0.029 for \MEACOO, 0.227 for \HCOthree, and 0.0087 for the aggregate \ce{MEA + MEAH+}.  Molecular \COtwo and \COthree are solved and plotted, but their residual rows are not used as major-species evidence.

The corresponding pressure baseline gives an overall median absolute $\log_{10}$ pressure residual of 0.160.  The temperature-resolved pressure comparison is uneven: the 40 and \SI{60}{\degreeCelsius} rows have larger residuals, whereas the 80, 100, and \SI{120}{\degreeCelsius} rows have smaller residuals.  The baseline is therefore used as a reproducibility and convention check, not as the final activity-based thermodynamic result.

\subsection{Activity-Coupled ePC-SAFT Evaluation}
The true-species ePC-SAFT activity calculation converged for all 161 assembled VLE records and all 74 speciation records.  The pressure residual is a median absolute $\log_{10}(P_{\mathrm{model}}/P_{\mathrm{data}})$ value of 0.493 across the pressure data.  Because \(10^{0.493}\approx 3.1\), the median pressure error corresponds to about a factor of 3.1.  This supports a diagnostic activity-based evaluation with quantified residuals, not a standalone process-design pressure correlation.

Temperature-resolved pressure errors remain largest between 40 and \SI{80}{\degreeCelsius} and decrease at \SI{120}{\degreeCelsius}.  The nonzero measured-target speciation metrics are 0.074 for \MEA, 0.033 for \MEAH, 0.040 for \MEACOO, 0.461 for \HCOthree, and 0.066 for the aggregate \ce{MEA + MEAH+}, all on the median absolute $\log_{10}(\mathrm{model}/\mathrm{data})$ scale.  Measurements reported as zero and treated as upper-bound targets remain below a maximum modeled \HCOthree mole fraction of 0.00128.  Carbonate nonzero measured targets have a larger median residual of 0.599 and are retained as a limiting species family.

\subsection{Direct Amine-Ion Regression Results}
The \MEAH and \MEACOO regression evidence supports the amine-family ion parameter choices more strongly than the carbonate-family and trace water-ion quantities.  The fit metrics reported in \cref{sec:data-methods} show small residual changes, so the result is used as parameter evidence rather than as a broad performance claim.

\subsection{Trace-Ion Sensitivity and Identifiability}
\HCOthree and \COthree are observed in the speciation data, but their parameter movement is less stable than the amine-family ions.  The regularized trace-carbonate fit retains \(d_{\HCOthree}^{\mathrm{Born}}=3.0\) and \(d_{\COthree}^{\mathrm{Born}}=3.0\), with a residual norm of 0.702.  A separate unanchored multistart check finds a lower residual near \(d_{\HCOthree}^{\mathrm{Born}}=6.80\) and \(d_{\COthree}^{\mathrm{Born}}=3.00\), decreasing the trace-carbonate residual norm to 0.683.  That alternative is not used in the reported activity-based evaluation because it is fitted only to carbonate-family residuals and is not coupled to the pressure/speciation objective.  \HthreeO and \Hydroxide are controlled mainly by charge balance and water autoionization and are not independently identifiable from the available NMR targets.

\subsection{Split Analysis and Transferability Limits}
The deterministic split analysis uses Aronu, Hilliard, Idris, and Mamun as pressure-training families with Jou and Xu held out for pressure validation; Matin and Boettinger are used for speciation training with Jakobsen held out for validation.  The held-out results show stronger transfer for directly fitted amine-family species than for carbonate-family species.  A reduced finite-difference sensitivity screen identifies \(k_{\MEA,\HtwoO}\), \(k_{\COtwo,\MEA}\), and \(d_{\COthree}^{\mathrm{Born}}\) as the strongest tracked directions in the reported objective neighborhood, while \(d_{\HthreeO}^{\mathrm{Born}}\) remains near-zero on the reported metrics.  These results indicate that additional pressure-weighted fitting should move binary interaction parameters and carbonate Born terms only under a multi-observable objective that preserves the reported amine-speciation behavior.
