\section{Conclusion}\label{sec:conclusion}

This work establishes the manuscript and modeling structure for an ePC-SAFT
description of the aqueous \MEA--\COtwo--\HtwoO system.  The current manuscript
organizes the experimental VLE and NMR speciation data into a consistent loading
convention, defines the reactive species basis needed for electrolyte modeling,
and demonstrates that the available raw data can be converted into stable
six-species and nine-species liquid composition paths.  These steps are
necessary prerequisites for fitting an electrolyte equation of state because
the pressure data alone cannot identify a physically meaningful liquid
speciation model.

The diagnostic results show that the legacy six-species chemistry reproduces
the expected pressure trend for 30 wt\% MEA and that the expanded nine-species
solver can resolve the charge-balanced liquid composition over the current
loading range without failed states.  The pressure diagnostics are not presented
as a final thermodynamic model.  Instead, they define the baseline behavior and
residual measures that the fitted ePC-SAFT model must improve.  The working
ePC-SAFT parameter folder already contains the required neutral and ionic
species entries, and the regression target has been formulated to combine
logarithmic \COtwo pressure residuals with direct liquid-speciation residuals.

Before submission, the remaining technical requirement is to insert the final
fitted ePC-SAFT parameter set and replace the diagnostic pressure/speciation
figures with the corresponding fitted comparisons.  Once those results are
added, the manuscript will be positioned to connect the thermodynamic model to
the absorption-column case study and to evaluate how electrolyte thermodynamics
affect the predicted operating behavior of an MEA-based carbon capture process.
